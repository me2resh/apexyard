## Card 1 — Taste resistance (GCC context)

**Headline:** Why “tasteless” is not a marketing trick in the Gulf

**Quote-style body (cite, do not attribute to a fake doctor):**

> In a study of 216 children in Jeddah, **89.8% resisted unfamiliar tastes** — the everyday barrier that makes gummies and chewables a daily battle. A powder that adds no taste, colour, or odour removes cooperation from the equation.

**Citation line:** Kutbi HA et al. · *Appetite* · 2019 · King Abdulaziz University, Jeddah · n=216 · Grade B

**Site use:** Hero proof hook · FAQ “Why powder?”

## Card 2 — Sugar in gummies vs 0g sachet

**Headline:** The hidden sugar cost of “kid vitamins”

**Body:**

> Leading gummy formats can deliver **~8g added sugar per dose** — before breakfast, that can approach **a third of a child’s daily added-sugar limit** (AHA guidance for children 2–18). blendavit is formulated with **0g added sugar** per sachet while still delivering a complete micronutrient panel.

**Citation line:** American Heart Association added sugars guidance · ConsumerLab 2023 category testing (potency accuracy) · Formulation design

**Site use:** Comparison table (EllaOla-style) · blendavit vs gummy row

## Card 3 — Iron in powder (technical moat)

**Headline:** Iron gummies can’t do what microencapsulated powder can

**Body:**

> Metallic taste is **unmaskable** in gelatin–sugar matrices — which is why most gummies **omit iron** entirely. **Lipofer® microencapsulated iron** is formulated to stay **undetectable** in food and drink while supplying iron in the same sachet as the rest of the panel.

**Citation line:** Layrisse M et al. · *J Nutr* · 2000 · liposomal iron absorption · Grade A · Hurrell RF & Egli IM · *Am J Clin Nutr* · 2010 · Grade A

**Site use:** Homepage second fold · Ingredients · “What makes us different”

**Stat callout (Kids / Toddlers PDP):**

> **3.3×** absorption vs conventional ferrous sulfate in published iron research — **zero GI events** in the liposomal arm (Layrisse 2000). GCC context: **~1 in 3 children under 5** are iron deficient (WHO/GBD).
